SOCIAL MEDIA STRATEGY

Platform Recommendations

Facebook

Primary platform for event announcements and community engagement.

  • Post 3-4 times per week
  • Share race results within 2 hours
  • Create event pages for special races
  • Engage with fan comments daily

Instagram

Visual platform for race day photos and driver spotlights.

  • Post 2-3 times per week
  • Share action shots and winner photos
  • Use Stories for behind-the-scenes
  • Utilize racing and local hashtags

Twitter

Real-time platform for race updates and results.

  • Live tweet during races
  • Share quick results immediately
  • Engage with racing community
  • Promote special events with countdown

Best Practices

  • Maintain consistent branding across all platforms
  • Respond to comments and messages within 24 hours
  • Tag sponsors and drivers in relevant posts
  • Use high-quality images that capture racing excitement
  • Create platform-specific content rather than cross-posting identical content
  • Analyze engagement metrics monthly and adjust strategy accordingly

EMAIL MARKETING STRATEGY

Campaign Types

Weekly Race Announcements

Send every Thursday to promote upcoming weekend races.

  • Featured race classes
  • Special events or promotions
  • Weather forecast
  • Ticket information

Race Results Recap

Send every Monday to summarize weekend results.

  • Top 3 finishers in each class
  • Highlight exciting moments
  • Driver quotes
  • Photo gallery link

Special Event Promotions

Send 2 weeks, 1 week, and 3 days before special events.

  • Event details and schedule
  • Special attractions
  • Early bird ticket offers
  • VIP package information

Sponsor Highlights

Send monthly to showcase sponsors.

  • Featured sponsor spotlight
  • Sponsor special offers
  • Sponsor events or news
  • Thank you message

Email Best Practices

  • Use mobile-responsive email templates
  • Keep subject lines under 50 characters
  • Include clear call-to-action buttons
  • Segment your audience based on interests (e.g., race classes)
  • Maintain consistent sending schedule
  • Include social media links in every email
  • Test different sending times to optimize open rates

EVENT PROMOTION STRATEGY

Promotion Timeline (Major Events)

  • 6-8 Weeks Out: Announce event date, save-the-date graphics, website update.
  • 4 Weeks Out: Detailed event information, ticket sales launch, social media campaign begins.
  • 2 Weeks Out: Email blast, press release, local media outreach, intensified social media.
  • 1 Week Out: Daily social media posts, countdown graphics, final email reminder.
  • Race Week: Increased social media engagement, behind-the-scenes content, final reminders.

Promotion Channels

  • Social Media: Facebook, Instagram, Twitter (organic and paid)
  • Email Marketing: Targeted campaigns to subscriber list
  • Website: Homepage banners, dedicated event pages, news articles
  • Local Media: Radio, newspapers, community calendars
  • Partnerships: Cross-promotion with sponsors and local businesses
  • On-Site Promotion: Announcements during regular race nights

SEASONAL PLANNING

Pre-Season (Jan-Mar)

  • Finalize race schedule and special events
  • Update website with new season information
  • Launch season ticket sales campaign
  • Secure sponsorships and partnerships
  • Plan major event promotion timelines
  • Create initial social media buzz for the upcoming season

In-Season (Apr-Sep)

  • Execute weekly marketing plan (social, email, website)
  • Promote special events according to timeline
  • Engage with fans and gather feedback
  • Highlight sponsors and drivers regularly
  • Capture high-quality photos and videos at each event
  • Monitor marketing performance and make adjustments

Post-Season (Oct-Dec)

  • Announce season champions and awards
  • Share season highlight reels and photo galleries
  • Conduct fan surveys for feedback
  • Begin planning for next season (schedule, sponsors)
  • Promote off-season events or merchandise sales
  • Maintain light social media presence to keep fans engaged
